About Synticore Website Compiler

A Note from the Author

Synticore Website Compiler started as a personal response to a simple problem: I wanted modern build automation without giving up direct control of my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Over time, it evolved into a reliable workflow for building static sites faster: automate repetitive tasks, keep output optimized, and stay close to the source. After seeing others ask for a similar approach, I decided to release Synticore publicly.

If you want a compiler that helps you move quickly while still keeping full ownership of your markup and structure, Synticore was made for that style of development. I hope it saves you time and gives you a clean, dependable path to production.

- James Steele Seeley

Inspiration

Synticore traces part of its early inspiration to the gulp-es6 project by Alessandro Castellani (Alecaddd) .
